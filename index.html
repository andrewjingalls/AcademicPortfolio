3D Printing

The specifications for this project were to design and 3D print anything we wanted. Essentially there were no specifications other than we had do model and print everything ourselves. I always loved the idea of a way to open my blinds in the morning without having to get out of bed. Instead of wasting money on some device that does exactly that, I spent countless hours designing and printing a device that (sort of) does that. I already had a small arduino controller and stepper motor that was controllable by an inferred remote. From then, I designed a track system with a gear that attached to the motor, and with the help of plenty of duct tape, stuck to the curtain rod and the wall. With the press of a button on the remote, the motor would turn the gear and in turn push and pull the curtain open and close. Admittedly, it turned out to be pretty anticlimactic as the curtain did not open very much, but the project still was a ton of fun to work on and a great learning experience. I became proficient in using my Prusa 3D printer, AutoDesk Fusion 360, and used my previous C++ knowledge to program the arduino and control the motor.

Data Science

For this project, I delved into the realm of sports analytics by focusing on NBA player data spanning the last two decades. With an aim to uncover insights and predict player performance, I employed machine learning models as the cornerstone of my analysis. This endeavor involved meticulously gathering and preprocessing vast datasets, encompassing player statistics, game outcomes, and various other relevant metrics. Leveraging sophisticated machine learning algorithms, I embarked on the task of constructing predictive models capable of discerning patterns and trends within the data. By training these models on historical player data, I aimed to forecast future player performances with a high degree of accuracy. This project not only enabled me to deepen my understanding of both sports analytics and machine learning methodologies but also presented an exciting opportunity to explore the intersection of data science and sports.

Data Structures and Algorithms

For this in depth project, I created a simulation of the classic game Super Mario! The simulation included a 5x5 grid which contained mushrooms, Goombas, Koopas, Coins, a level Boss, and Mario depicted by a "H" for Hero. The simulation was created using C++ and was designed for Mario to randomly move around the grid and interact with the other objects. If Mario encountered a mushroom, his power level would increase and if he got a high enough power level, would increase a life. If Mario encountered a Goomba, Koopa, or level boss, there was an algorithm that would determine if Mario would win or lose the fight. Mario also had to ability to pick up coins and even could find Warp Pipes to skip levels. The goal was to make it through both levels and defeat the final boss. This project was a great way to learn about 2D arrays, pointers, and gain a deeper understanding into object oriented programming.
